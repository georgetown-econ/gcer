--- layout: post title: "Texas Advanced Computing Center highlights GCER Fellow Mark Huggett's work on taxing top earners" date: 2014-10-20 00:00:00 +UTC ---

GCER Fellow Mark Huggett and his co-author Alejandro Badal, who is an economist at the Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis, used one of the world's most powerful supercomputers at the Texas Advanced Computing Center@@@ (TACC) to simulate the behavior of millions of model households. "When one builds an economic model, a typical assumption is that agents make best choices to maximize their welfare", says Huggett. "Solving a maximization problem by computer can take a particularly long time if the number of possible states of individuals are very large".

To address this computational challenge, Badel and Huggett turned to Extreme Science and Engineering Discovery Environment (XSEDE) at the TACC to run simulations of their model economy.
